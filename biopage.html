My Path to UX

life lessons along the way

I believe the right thinking is very important, no matter if it’s engineering, the humanities, or design. And my transition from architecture to UX played a major role at shaping this. Here are 3 life lessons that this transition has taught me.

Lesson #1 - Self Realization

A career requires both passion and belief. It’s like a bike, when you got the two wheels under you, you move easier and faster, also with joy. I found my passion for design in architecture, but I found my belief from ux design.

Lesson #2 - Growth Mindset

Humans can iterate just like how products do. Inspired from the Design of Everyday Things and the Principles, I feel an alignment of work and life by pursuing UX.

Lesson #3 - Embrace Reality

UX design is Architecture in the new era. Inspired by a lot of factors. The progress of urbanization in the last hundreds of years enabled high frequency of spatial interaction in the cities, reducing population of the countryside that were poor at enabling human interaction. Similarly, sensations and interactions are turning from cities to the Internet. Internet is becoming the new City, and cities are destined to be the new countryside.
